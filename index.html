Welcome to information about Mary Allen Wilkes

"We had the quaint notion at the time that software should be completely, absolutely free of bugs. Unfortunately it's a notion that never really quite caught on."

Early Career

After graduating from Wellesley College, Mary Allen Wilkes began her career at MIT, despite having no prior programming experience. Her entry into computer science was pioneering at a time when programming was a fledgling discipline.

Pioneering Work

Mary was instrumental in the development of the LINC (Laboratory Instrument Computer), one of the first personal computers. She is credited as the first person to use a personal computer at home, which she helped build and program herself.

Legacy

Mary co-authored the seminal guide, "Programming the LINC," laying the foundation for personal computing. Her contributions have inspired generations of women in technology and showcased the potential of computing in everyday life.

Breaking Through

Joined a male-dominated field and excelled without prior experience.

Pioneering Innovation

The first person to use a home computer that she built herself.

Co-Authoring

Co-authored "Programming the LINC" . Influenced future innovations in the industry.

Additional Information

Mary Allen Wilkes, a pioneering computer programmer, co-authored Programming the LINC, shaping early computer programming. She was also the first to use a home-built computer, influencing future technological advancements.
